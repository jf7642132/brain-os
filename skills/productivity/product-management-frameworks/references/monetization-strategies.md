# Monetization Strategies — Complete Reference

**Source**: phuryn/pm-skills → pm-product-strategy → monetization-strategy

## 7 Monetization Models

| Model | How It Works | Best Fit | Key Risk | Experiment |
|-------|-------------|----------|----------|------------|
| **Freemium** | Free core + paid premium features | High-volume, low-touch (design, productivity) | Low conversion (1-5%) | Launch freemium, track conversion, gather upgrade feedback |
| **Subscription (monthly/annual)** | Recurring charge for ongoing access | Continuous-value products (software, platforms) | Churn; cannibalization annual vs monthly | Offer to beta customers, measure churn and NPS |
| **Usage-based** | Pay per call, storage, transaction | B2B platforms, APIs, variable-need services | Unpredictable revenue; customer cost anxiety | Implement tracking, pilot with 5-10 beta customers, model revenue |
| **Enterprise / Seat-based** | Price per user, department, or seat | B2B SaaS with team adoption | Sales complexity, contract length | 5-10 customer interviews to validate per-seat pricing & support model |
| **One-time purchase** | Single upfront fee for permanent license | Niche products, tools, templates (not ongoing services) | Revenue concentrated at launch; no recurring revenue | Launch limited offering, track conversion & satisfaction |
| **Marketplace / transaction fee** | Percentage or fixed fee from transactions | Platforms connecting supply & demand | Chicken-and-egg liquidity; trust & safety | MVP with limited sellers, free period to drive supply, model unit economics |
| **Advertising / sponsorship** | Revenue from ads, sponsored content, brand partnerships | High-traffic, consumer-facing products | Brand damage from intrusive ads; UX degradation | Test ads with small user segment, measure engagement & revenue |

## Evaluation Framework (per strategy)

For each candidate strategy, assess:
1. **Strategy Name & Description** — model, how it works, who pays and what they get
2. **How It Works** — revenue model, pricing mechanics, value exchange, payment frequency & size
3. **Audience Fit** — why it resonates, alignment with needs, problems solved, addressable market
4. **Unit Economics** — estimated CAC, LTV, break-even timeline, target gross margin
5. **Risks & Challenges** — market adoption, price sensitivity, competitive vulnerability, churn
6. **Competitive Position** — competitor monetization, differentiation, switching barriers
7. **Validation Experiment** — low-cost test, success metric, timeline & resources

## 6-Step Process
1. Brainstorm 3-5 distinct strategies
2. For each: describe how it works, assess audience fit, outline risks, estimate unit economics
3. Design a low-effort validation experiment for each
4. Prioritize by: strategic fit, ease of implementation, market validation potential, competitive advantage
5. Recommend 1-2 to test first
6. Create testing roadmap with success criteria

## Key Principles
- Best monetization aligns with customer value and willingness to pay
- Test early and often; don't wait for a perfect product to validate pricing
- Most successful products use hybrid models
- Pricing can be changed; not implementing monetization cannot
