# Beachhead Segment Selection

**Framework**: Geoffrey Moore, *Crossing the Chasm*
**Application**: Choosing the first target market segment for product launch

## 4 Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Burning Pain Point 🔥
- Acute, unmet problem
- Daily frustration, significant productivity loss or cost
- Emotional urgency
- Expensive/fragile workarounds
- Problem getting worse over time

**Signal strength**: Customer actively searching for solutions, has tried multiple workarounds

### 2. Willingness to Pay 💰
- Budget allocation exists
- Clear ROI (value > cost)
- Economic impact justifies cost
- Decision-maker autonomy (no chain approvals)
- No fully satisfying free/DIY alternatives

**Signal strength**: Customer asks about pricing before seeing demo

### 3. Winnable Market Share 🏆
- Target 60-70% of segment in 3-18 months
- Large enough to matter but not oversaturated
- Limited competition or easy differentiation
- Fragmented/complacent existing players
- Clear competitive advantage

**Signal strength**: You can name every competitor; they all have the same blind spot

### 4. Referral Potential 🔁
- Customers naturally refer to peers
- Professional communities exist
- Interaction with adjacent segments
- High word-of-mouth culture
- Network effects possible
- Solving one creates demand in adjacent segments

**Signal strength**: A success story in this segment opens the next segment naturally

## 6-Step Selection Process

1. **List Potential Segments** — verticals, company size, job titles, geography, use cases, maturity
2. **Research Pain Points** — interviews, surveys, analyst reports, competitor reviews; quantify cost/impact; identify workarounds
3. **Assess Willingness to Pay** — budget, ROI, current spending, decision process, deal size, pricing sensitivity
4. **Evaluate Winnability** — TAM, competitive landscape, differentiation, distribution, time/resources, market growth
5. **Identify Referral Pathways** — adjacent segments, network effects, communities, expansion path, viral effects
6. **Select Beachhead** — highest combined score, most achievable with current resources, fastest path to PMF & revenue, best reference for adjacent expansion

## Output Template
- Top 3-5 segments with scoring
- Primary recommendation
- Pain point validation & evidence
- Willingness to pay assessment & pricing guidance
- Market share & revenue projections
- Referral & expansion pathways to adjacent segments
- 90-day customer acquisition plan for beachhead
- Post-beachhead expansion roadmap

## Key Tips
- **Start absurdly specific** — niche > vague mass market
- Choose segment **most likely to evangelize** your solution
- **Validate all four criteria** with **at least 10 customer interviews**
- Select **fastest path to revenue & references**
- Ensure beachhead can **reference to adjacent segments**
- **Focus all resources** on dominating (not diluting)
- Plan exit only after **60%+ market share**

## Application: Chemical Trade Intelligence System

Potential beachhead options to evaluate:
- **Tier**: Small/medium chemical traders vs. large state-owned enterprises vs. chemical manufacturers
- **Geography**: East China (Jiangsu/Zhejiang) vs. South China (Guangdong) vs. specific city
- **Product category**: Fine chemicals vs. bulk chemicals vs. specialty chemicals
- **Persona**: Procurement managers vs. export sales directors vs. risk compliance officers

Most promising hypothesis: **SME chemical export managers in East China** — they face daily pricing volatility, have no Bloomberg-level tools, work through WeChat groups, and have budget discretion.
